About

Though there have been many advances in robotic technology, we still do not have robots that can perform common household tasks like empty a dishwasher, or tidy up a room by putting scattered objects in their proper places. This workshop will focus on robots for doing household chores. We will consider six household tasks, like doing dishes and tidying up, and identify the technologies needed for enabling a robot to perform them. These technologies will necessarily be multidisciplinary and multimodal, including object recognition, extraction of affordances, grasping, planning robot movement, multimodal human-robot communication involving speech, gaze, and gestures, and so on. The mode of the workshop will be based on short presentations, brainstorming sessions in breakout groups, and group discussion. At the end, we expect to identify key challenges that must be addressed to have robots perform household chores.

Call for papers

In this workshop, we aim to bring together machine learning and robotics researchers who work at the intersection of these fields. We invite researchers to submit work in the following or related areas Please submit a 2-4 page position paper which should highlight: 1) Some robotic technology you are developing, and 2) potential use of this technology for enabling a robot to do some household chore. The second part is very crucial in deciding which submissions will be accepted.
