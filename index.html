About

This workshop is aimed at exploring multimodal approaches for household automation. While vacuum robots are already present in many households, many common chores are easy for humans, such as emptying a dishwasher, but pose a tough challenge to robots. We plan to discuss how to meet such challenges, not only in their robotic aspects but also regarding human-robot (verbal and non-verbal) communication (telling a robot where to stack the dishes), and ethnographic considerations (being sensitive cultural and personal preferences of the user.) This workshop intends to bring together experts in different disciplines, such as grasping, planning, computer vision, reinforcement learning, human-robot interaction, sensors, AI processors, and multimodal human-robot communication involving speech, gaze, and gestures. We will provide a platform for researchers in robotics, AI, computer vision, cognitive science, and social science to present their work and exchange ideas and opinions. To actively involve participants in the workshop, we will use a multi-format organization that combines invited talks, short presentations, brainstorming sessions, and a panel discussion. At the end of the workshop, we aim to identify critical challenges that must be addressed to have robots perform household chores and broaden our understanding of other related discipline.
